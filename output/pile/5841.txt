    "text": "Developers Payce Consolidated and Sekisui House have submitted a long awaited development proposal for one of Brisbane\u2019s most premium development sites along Boundary Street in West End.\n\nFormerly housing the Peters Ice Cream factory, West Village is a masterplanned urban community housing 1350 apartments, a 4500sqm supermarket, 500sqm of community facilities, a 450-space public car park, and parking for 1600 bicycles.\n\nThe mixed use $800 million precinct is broken up into three stages and includes seven residential buildings of varying heights:\n\nStage 1 \u2013 A code assessable development application over an area being greater than 10,000m2 for 2 multiple dwelling buildings being less than or equal to 15 storeys above a retail / commercial podium and basement car parking.\n\nStage 1A \u2013 An impact assessable development application within the Heritage Curtilage involving the adaptive retail / commercial reuse of the existing local heritage buildings, village green and retail pods.\n\nStage 2 \u2013 An impact assessable development application for 3 multiple dwelling buildings ranging from less than or equal to 15 storeys to no greater than 20 storeys above a retail / commercial podium which includes an individual tenancy of no greater than 4,500m2 gross floor area being for a super market tenancy, parking station and basement car parking.\n\nStage 3 \u2013 A code assessable development application for two (2) Multiple Dwelling buildings ranging from less than or equal to 19 storeys and no greater than 25 storeys above a Retail / Commercial podium and basement car parking.\n\nAccording to the DA, the masterplan boasts the following design principals:\n\nOur Big 5 Design Moves\n\nEmbodying the aspirations set by the community & committed to by the project team, these key moves aim to celebrate West Village as the centre of community life in West End.\n\n1 Embrace Boundary St\n\nBoundary St has long been the centre of life in West End.\n\nOpening up the site & embracing Boundary St will create a seamless extension of the public realm allowing its High Street to expand into a lush, sub-tropical community heart at West Village.\n\n2 Maximise Open Space\n\nWest Ender\u2019s & Brisbane urbanites relish in their relaxed outdoor lifestyle.\n\nRe-imagining the precinct as a cathedral in the round \u2013 with two striking heritage buildings surrounded by open public space. Places to sit, gather, relax and celebrate local events \u2013 in lush sub- tropical gardens with views to our big blue sky.\n\n3 Celebrate Pedestrian Connections\n\nExtending through the site with unique laneways, paths, squares & stairs\n\nUnderstanding the character of West End & heritage fabric of the site, each of the 6 laneway connections is celebrated to create a series of unique experiences which connect West End.\n\n4 Reinterpret The Site\u2019s Existing Hill\n\nCascading public realm which appropriates the ridge line & natural fall across the precinct.\n\nA precinct landscape that reinterprets the existing hill \u2013 framing views, reinstating green, providing a landscape of discovery. \u2018The Steps\u2019 becoming West End\u2019s own Spanish Steps, a place to meet, sit, watch the world go by.\n\n5 Benchmark Sub-Tropical Gardens\n\nIn an area with beloved parks & riverscapes, this is West End\u2019s central urban garden.\n\nWith people and public space at the fore, West Village will provide West End with benchmark sub-tropical landscapes, a series of continuous green corridors through the precinct and a lush, garden setting for West End\u2019s heart.\n\nThe masterplan seeks to create 9 new public spaces:\n\n6 New Laneways\n\nFactory Lane, Gun Shop Lane, Little Jane Lane, Mollison Lane, Peters Lane, Wilson Lane\n\nWith newly restored industrial edges, Factory lane picks up the industrial aesthetic and becomes WV\u2019s chic, authentic working lane. Think Flinders Lane Melbourne \u2013 small bars, up & coming restaurants, gallery openings which spill onto the laneway at night. It\u2019s flexible design allows for markets, festivals, temporary events- Factory Lane is the spine WV\u2019s laneway events spill from.\n\n2 Squares\n\nThe Common Peter\u2019s Square:\n\nIts vibrant open frontage embraces Boundary Street. Flanked with cafes, bars and the newly restored Ice Cream Factory, West End locals and Brisbane urbanites meander along shaded paths on their way to grab a coffee, buy fresh bread or just watch life pass by from the shade of one of West Villages beloved Fig Trees.\n\nRich with activity and texture and surrounded by terracing, Peters Square is the contained working heart of West Village. Flanked with retail and fresh produce, this is the market square fine-tuned to our climate and lifestyle. Shaded seating with views across the square and up the laneways are the perfect place to meet up with friends, or stop and have an ice cream.\n\nThe Steps\n\nWest End\u2019s Spanish Steps\n\nThe Master Plan:\n\nPublic Realm Vision:\n\nThe DA number for this development is A004115562.\n\nImages supplied by DA document."
