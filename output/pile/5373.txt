    "text": "---\nabstract: 'We investigate the notion of Connes-amenability, introduced by Runde in [@Runde1], for bidual algebras and weighted semigroup algebras. We provide some simplifications to the notion of a $\\sigma WC$-virtual diagonal, as introduced in [@Runde2], especially in the case of the bidual of an Arens regular Banach algebra. We apply these results to discrete, weighted, weakly cancellative semigroup algebras, showing that these behave in the same way as C$^*$-algebras with regards Connes-amenability of the bidual algebra. We also show that for each one of these cancellative semigroup algebras $l^1(S,\\omega)$, we have that $l^1(S,\\omega)$ is Connes-amenable (with respect to the canonical predual $c_0(S)$) if and only if $l^1(S,\\omega)$ is amenable, which is in turn equivalent to $S$ being an amenable group. This latter point was first shown by Gr[\u00f6]{}nb\u00e6k in [@Gron1], but we provide a unified proof. Finally, we consider the homological notion of injectivity, and show that here, weighted semigroup algebras do not behave like C$^*$-algebras.'\nauthor:\n- Matthew Daws\ntitle: '<span style=\"font-variant:small-caps;\">Connes-amenability of bidual and weighted semigroup algebras</span>'\n---\n\n*2000 Mathematics Subject Classification:* 22D15, 43A20, 46H25, 46H99 (primary), 46E15, 46M20, 47B47.\n\nIntroduction\n============\n\nWe first fix some notation, following [@Dales]. For a Banach space $E$, we let $E'$ be its dual space, and for $\\mu\\in E'$ and $x\\in E$, we write ${{\\langle {\\mu} , {x} \\rangle}} = \\mu(x)$ for notational convenience. We then have the canonical map $\\kappa_E:E\\rightarrow E''$ defined by ${{\\langle {\\kappa_E(x)} , {\\mu} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\mu} , {x} \\rangle}}$ for $\\mu\\in E',x\\in E$. For Banach spaces $E$ and $F$, we write ${\\mathcal{B}}(E,F)$ for the Banach space of bounded linear maps between $E$ and $F$. We write ${\\mathcal{B}}(E,E) = {\\mathcal{B}}(E)$. For $T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(E,F)$, the *adjoint* of $T$ is $T'\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(F',E')$, defined by ${{\\langle {T'(\\mu)} , {x} \\rangle}} =\n{{\\langle {\\mu} , {T(x)} \\rangle}}$, for $\\mu\\in F'$ and $x\\in E$.\n\nLet ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a Banach algebra. A *Banach left ${\\mathcal{A}}$-module* is a Banach space $E$ together with a bilinear map ${\\mathcal{A}}\\times E \\rightarrow E; (a,x) \\mapsto a\\cdot x$, such that $\\|a\\cdot x\\|\\leq \\|a\\|\\|x\\|$ and $a\\cdot(b\\cdot x)=\nab\\cdot x$ for $a,b\\in{\\mathcal{A}}$ and $x\\in E$. Similarly, we have the notion of a *Banach right ${\\mathcal{A}}$-module* and a *Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule*. If $E$ is a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule (resp. left or right module) then ${\\mathcal{A}}'$ is a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule (resp. right or left module) with module action given by $${{\\langle {a\\cdot\\mu} , {x} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\mu} , {x\\cdot a} \\rangle}}\n\\qquad {{\\langle {\\mu\\cdot a} , {x} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\mu} , {a\\cdot x} \\rangle}}\n\\qquad (a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}, x\\in E).$$ Notice that as ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is certainly a bimodule over itself (with module action induced by the algebra product) we also have that ${\\mathcal{A}}'$, ${\\mathcal{A}}''$ etc. are Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodules. Given a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule $E$, a subspace $F$ of $E$ is a *submodule* if $a\\cdot x, x\\cdot a\\in F$ for each $a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}$ and $x\\in F$. For Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodules $E$ and $F$, $T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(E,F)$ is an *${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule homomorphism* when $$a \\cdot T(x) = T(a\\cdot x) \\qquad\nT(x) \\cdot a = T(x\\cdot a) \\qquad (a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}, x\\in E).$$\n\nA linear map $d:{\\mathcal{A}} \\rightarrow E$ between a Banach algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$ and a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule $E$ is a *derivation* if $d(ab) = a \\cdot d(b) + d(a)\\cdot b$ for $a,b\\in{\\mathcal{A}}$. For $x\\in E$, we define $\\delta_x : {\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow E$ by $\\delta_x(a) =\na\\cdot x-x\\cdot a$. Then $\\delta_x$ is a derivation, called an *inner derivation*.\n\nA Banach algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is said to be *super-amenable* or *contractable* if every bounded derivation $d:{\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow E$, for every Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule $E$, is inner. For example, a C$^*$-algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is super-amenable if and only if ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is finite-dimensional. It is conjectured that there are no infinite-dimensional, super-amenable Banach algebras.\n\nIf we restrict to derivations to $E'$ for Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodules $E$ then we arrive at the notion of *amenability*. For example, a C$^*$-algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is amenable if and only if ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is nuclear; a group algebra $L^1(G)$ is amenable if and only if the locally compact group $G$ is amenable (which is the motivating example). See [@RundeBook] for further discussions of amenability and related notions.\n\nLet $E$ be a Banach space and $F$ a closed subspace of $E$. Then we naturally, isometrically, identify $F'$ with $E' / F^\\circ$, where $$F^\\circ = \\{ \\mu\\in E' : {{\\langle {\\mu} , {x} \\rangle}}=0 \\ (x\\in F) \\}.$$\n\n\\[dual\\_means\\] Let $E$ be a Banach space and $E_*$ be a closed subspace of $E'$. Let $\\pi_{E_*}: E'' \\rightarrow E''/E_*^\\circ$ be the quotient map, and suppose that $\\pi_{E_*} \\circ \\kappa_E$ is an isomorphism from $E$ to $E_*'$. Then we say that $E$ is a *dual Banach space* with *predual $E_*$*.\n\nWhen ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is a dual Banach space with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}_*$ which is also a submodule of ${\\mathcal{A}}'$ we say that ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is a *dual Banach algebra*. [$\\square$]{}\n\nFor a dual Banach algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$ with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}_*$, we henceforth identify ${\\mathcal{A}}$ with ${\\mathcal{A}}_*'$. Thus we get a weak$^*$-topology on ${\\mathcal{A}}$, which we denote by $\\sigma({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*)$. It is a simple exercise to show that ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is a dual Banach algebra if and only if ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is a dual Banach space such that the algebra product is separately $\\sigma({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*)$-continuous (see [@Runde1]). The following lemma is standard.\n\n\\[weak\\_star\\_cts\\] Let $E$ and $F$ be dual Banach spaces with preduals $E_*$ and $F_*$ respectively, and let $T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(E,F)$. For von Neumann algebras, which are the motivating example of dual Banach algebras, there is a weaker notion of amenablity, called Connes-amenability, which has a natural generalisation to the case of dual Banach algebras.\n\nLet ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a dual Banach algebra with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}_*$. Let $E$ be a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule. Then $E'$ is a *w$^*$-Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule* if, for each $\\mu\\in E'$, the maps $${\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow E',\\quad a\\mapsto\n\\begin{cases} a\\cdot \\mu, \\\\ \\mu\\cdot a \\end{cases}$$ are $\\sigma({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*) - \\sigma(E',E)$ continuous.\n\nThen $({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*)$ is Connes-amenable if, for each w$^*$-Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule $E'$, each derivation $d:{\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow E'$, which is $\\sigma({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*) -\n\\sigma(E',E)$ continuous, is inner. [$\\square$]{}\n\nGiven a Banach algebra ${\\mathcal{A}}$, we define bilinear maps ${\\mathcal{A}}''\\times{\\mathcal{A}}'\\rightarrow{\\mathcal{A}}'$ and ${\\mathcal{A}}'\\times{\\mathcal{A}}''\\rightarrow{\\mathcal{A}}'$ by $${{\\langle {\\Phi\\cdot\\mu} , {a} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\Phi} , {\\mu\\cdot a} \\rangle}}\n\\quad {{\\langle {\\mu\\cdot\\Phi} , {a} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\Phi} , {a\\cdot\\mu} \\rangle}}\n\\qquad (\\Phi\\in{\\mathcal{A}}'', \\mu\\in{\\mathcal{A}}', a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}).$$ We then define two bilinear maps ${\\Box},{\\Diamond}:{\\mathcal{A}}''\\times{\\mathcal{A}}''\n\\rightarrow{\\mathcal{A}}''$ by $${{\\langle {\\Phi{\\Box}\\Psi} , {\\mu} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\Phi} , {\\Psi\\cdot\\mu} \\rangle}}\n\\quad {{\\langle {\\Phi{\\Diamond}\\Psi} , {\\mu} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {\\Psi} , {\\mu\\cdot\\Phi} \\rangle}}\n\\qquad (\\Phi,\\Psi\\in{\\mathcal{A}}'', \\mu\\in{\\mathcal{A}}').$$ We can check that ${\\Box}$ and ${\\Diamond}$ are actually algebra products, called the *first* and *second Arens products* respectively. Then $\\kappa_A:{\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow{\\mathcal{A}}''$ is a homomorphism with respect to either Arens product. When ${\\Box}= {\\Diamond}$, we say that ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is *Arens regular*. In particular, when ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is Arens regular, we may check that ${\\mathcal{A}}''$ is a dual Banach algebra with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}'$.\n\n\\[ca\\_facts\\] Let ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be an Arens regular Banach algebra. Then ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is Arens regular, and ${\\mathcal{A}}''$ is Connes-amenable if and only if ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is amenable.\n\nThe first statements are [@Runde1 Corollary\u00a04.3] and [@Runde1 Theorem\u00a04.4]. The statement about C$^*$-algebras is detailed in [@RundeBook Chapter\u00a06].\n\nAnother class of Connes-amenable dual Banach algebras is given by Runde in [@Runde4], where it is shown that $M(G)$, the measure algebra of a locally compact group $G$, is amenable if and only if $G$ is amenable.\n\nThe organisation of this paper is as follows. Firstly, we study intrinsic characterisations of amenability, recalling a result of Runde from [@Runde2]. We then simplify these conditions in the case of Arens regular Banach algebras. We recall the notion of an *injective* module, and quickly note how Connes-amenability can be phrased in this language. The final section of the paper then applies these ideas to weighted semigroup algebras. We finish with some open questions.\n\nCharacterisations of amenability\n================================\n\nLet $E$ and $F$ be Banach spaces, and form the algebraic tensor product $E\\otimes F$. We can norm $E\\otimes F$ with the *projective tensor norm*, defined as $$\\|u\\|_\\pi = \\inf\\Big\\{ \\sum_{k=1}^n \\|x_k\\|\\|y_k\\| :\nu = \\sum_{k=1}^n x_k \\otimes y_k \\Big\\}\n\\qquad (u\\in E\\otimes F).$$ Then the completion of $(E\\otimes F, \\|\\cdot\\|_\\pi)$ is $E {{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}F$, the *projective tensor product* of $E$ and $F$.\n\nLet ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a Banach algebra. Then ${\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}}$ is a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule for the module actions given by $$a\\cdot (b\\otimes c) = ab \\otimes c,\n\\quad (b\\otimes c) \\cdot a = b\\otimes ca\n\\qquad (a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}, b\\otimes c\\in{\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}}).$$ Define $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}} : {\\mathcal{A}} {{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}} \\rightarrow {\\mathcal{A}}$ by $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}(a\\otimes b)=ab$. Then $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}$ is an ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule homomorphism.\n\n\\[when\\_amen\\] Let ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a Banach algebra. Then the following are equivalent:\n\n1.  ${\\mathcal{A}}$ is amenable;\n\n2.  ${\\mathcal{A}}$ has a *virtual diagonal*, which is a functional $M\\in ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})''$ such that $a\\cdot M =M\\cdot a$ and $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}''(M) \\cdot a = \\kappa_{{\\mathcal{A}}}(a)$ for each $a\\in{\\mathcal{A}}$.\n\n[$\\square$]{}\n\nRunde introduced, in [@Runde2], the following notion in order to prove a version of the above theorem for Connes-amenability.\n\nLet ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a dual Banach algebra with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}_*$, and let $E$ be a Banach ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule. Then $x\\in \\sigma WC(E)$ if and only if the maps ${\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow E$, $$a \\mapsto \\begin{cases} a\\cdot x, \\\\ x\\cdot a \\end{cases}$$ are $\\sigma({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}_*) - \\sigma(E,E')$ continuous. [$\\square$]{}\n\nIt is clear that $\\sigma WC(E)$ is a closed submodule of $E$. The ${\\mathcal{A}}$-bimodule homomorphism $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}$ has adjoint $\\Delta'_{{\\mathcal{A}}}:{\\mathcal{A}}' \\rightarrow ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})'$. In [@Runde2 Corollary\u00a04.6] it is shown that $\\Delta'_{{\\mathcal{A}}}({\\mathcal{A}}_*) \\subseteq \\sigma WC( ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})' )$. Consequently, we can view $\\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}'$ as a map ${\\mathcal{A}}_* \\rightarrow \\sigma WC( ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})' )$, and hence view $\\Delta''_{{\\mathcal{A}}}$ as a map $\\sigma WC( ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})' )' \\rightarrow {\\mathcal{A}}_*'\n= {\\mathcal{A}}$, denoted by $\\tilde \\Delta_{{\\mathcal{A}}}$.\n\n\\[Runde\\_Thm\\] Let ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a dual Banach algebra with predual ${\\mathcal{A}}_*$. We write ${\\mathcal{W}}(E,F)$ for the collection of weakly-compact operators in ${\\mathcal{B}}(E,F)$.\n\n\\[wsw\\_cty\\] Let $E$ be a dual Banach space with predual $E_*$, let $F$ be a Banach space, and let $T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(E,F')$. Then the following are equivalent, and in particular each imply that $T$ is weakly-compact:\n\n1.  $T$ is $\\sigma(E,E_*) - \\sigma(F',F'')$ continuous;\n\n2.  $T'(F'') \\subseteq \\kappa_{E_*}(E_*)$;\n\n3.  there exists $S\\in{\\mathcal{W}}(F,E_*)$ such that $S' = T$.\n\nThat (1) and (2) are equivalent is standard (compare with Lemma\u00a0\\[weak\\_star\\_cts\\]).\n\nSuppose that (2) holds, so that we may define $S\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(F,E_*)$ by $\\kappa_{E_*}\\circ S = T'\\circ\\kappa_F$. Then, for $x\\in E$ and $y\\in F$, we have $${{\\langle {x} , {S(y)} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {T'(\\kappa_F(y))} , {x} \\rangle}}\n= {{\\langle {T(x)} , {y} \\rangle}},$$ so that $S'=T$. Then $S''(F'') = T'(F'') \\subseteq\n\\kappa_{E_*}(E_*)$, so that $S$ is weakly-compact, by Gantmacher\u2019s Theorem, so that (3) holds.\n\nConversely, if (3) holds, as $S$ is weakly-compact, we have $\\kappa_{E*}(E_*) \\supseteq S''(F'') = T'(F'')$, so that (2) holds.\n\nIt is standard that for Banach spaces $E$ and $F$, we have $(E{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}F)' = {\\mathcal{B}}(F,E')$ with duality defined by $${{\\langle {T} , {x\\otimes y} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {T(y)} , {x} \\rangle}}\n\\qquad (T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}(F,E'), x\\otimes y\\in E{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}F).$$ Then we see, for $a,b,c\\in{\\mathcal{A}}$ and $T\\in({\\mathcal{A}}\n{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})' = {\\mathcal{B}}({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}')$, that ${{\\langle {a\\cdot T} , {b\\otimes c} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {T(ca)} , {b} \\rangle}}$ and that ${{\\langle {T\\cdot a} , {b\\otimes c} \\rangle}} = {{\\langle {T(c)} , {ab} \\rangle}}\n= {{\\langle {T(c)\\cdot a} , {b} \\rangle}}$ so that $$(a\\cdot T)(c) = T(ca), \\quad (T\\cdot a)(c) = T(c)\\cdot a\n\\qquad (a,c\\in{\\mathcal{A}}, T:{\\mathcal{A}}\\rightarrow{\\mathcal{A}}').\n\\label{eq:one}$$\n\nNotice that we could also have defined $(E{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}F)'$ to be ${\\mathcal{B}}(E,F')$. [$\\square$]{}\n\n\\[wap\\_to\\_maps\\] Let ${\\mathcal{A}}$ be a Banach algebra, and let $T\\in{\\mathcal{B}}({\\mathcal{A}},{\\mathcal{A}}')\n= ({\\mathcal{A}}{{\\widehat{\\otimes}}}{\\mathcal{A}})'$.
