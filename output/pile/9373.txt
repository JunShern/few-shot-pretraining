Jos\u00e9 dos Campos, SP, Brasil\ntitle: '**Relativistic Bound States in 2+1 and 1+1 Dimensions in the Null-Plane [^1]**'\n---\n\n=cmr8\n\n1.5pt\n\n\\#1[/\\#1]{}\n\nThe Faddeev-like equation for the component of the three-boson vertex for a relativistic contact interaction is [@fred92] $$\\begin{aligned}\nv(q^\\mu)=2\\tau(M_2) \\int \\frac{d^nk}{(2\\pi)^n}\n\\frac{i}{k^2-M^2+i{\\varepsilon}} \\frac{i}{(P_3-q-k)^2-M^2+i{\\varepsilon}}v(k^\\mu),\n\\label{1}\\end{aligned}$$ where $P^\\mu_3=(M_{3B},0,0,0)$ is the three-boson four-momentum in the center of mass system, $n$ is the dimension of the space-time, the mass of the two-boson subsystem is given by $M_2^2=(P_3-q)^2$ and the single boson mass is $M$. The factor 2 comes from symmetrization of the total vertex.\n\nThe total three-boson vertex is the sum of three Faddeev components in which each boson is spectator once [@fred92]. The two-boson scattering amplitude, $\\tau(M_2)$, enters in the kernel of the integral equation for the vertex in Eq.(\\[1\\]). It is easily obtained as: $$\\begin{aligned}\n\\tau^{(n)}(M_2)= \\left\\{i\\lambda^{-1} - B^{(n)}(M_2)\\right\\}^{-1}, \n\\label{2}\\end{aligned}$$ where $\\lambda$ is the coupling constant of the zero-range interaction, $M_2$ is the mass of the two boson system and $B(M_2)$ is the kernel of the integral equation for the scattering amplitude $$\\begin{aligned}\n B^{(n)}(M_2)=-\\int \\frac{d^nk}{(2\\pi)^n}\n\\left\\{\\left(k^2-M^2+i{\\varepsilon}\\right)\\left((P-k)^2-M^2+i{\\varepsilon}\\right)\n\\right\\}^{-1},\n\\label{3}\\end{aligned}$$ where $M$ is the boson mass and $P^\\mu=(M_2,0,0,0)$ .\n\nThe value of $\\lambda$ is chosen such that the two-boson system has one bound-state. The scattering amplitude, Eq.(\\[2\\]), has a pole at the bound-state mass $(M_{2B})$, which yields $$\\begin{aligned}\ni\\lambda^{-1}= B^{(n)}(M_{2B}). \n\\label{4}\\end{aligned}$$\n\nIn two dimensions the two-boson scattering amplitude for $M_2 \\ < \\ 2M$ is $$\\begin{aligned}\n\\tau^{(2)}(M_2) = - 2\\pi i \\left\\{ \n\\frac{atan\\left(2\\beta(M_{2B})\\right)^{-1}}\n{M^{2}_{2B}\\beta(M_{2B})}\n - \\frac{atan\\left(2\\beta(M_{2})\\right)^{-1}} \n{M^{2}_{2}\\beta(M_{2})}\n\\right\\}^{-1} \\ ,\n\\label{5}\\end{aligned}$$ where $ \\beta(M_2)=\\sqrt{\\frac{M^2}{M_{2}^2}-\\frac14}$ .\n\nIn three dimensions the scattering amplitude is $$\\begin{aligned}\n\\tau^{(3)}(M_2)=- 8\\pi i \\left\\{ M^{-1}_{2B} \nln\\left(\\frac{2M+M_{2B}}{2M-M_{2B}} \n\\right)\n-M^{-1}_{2} ln\\left(\\frac{2M+M_{2}}{2M-M_{2}} \n\\right)\n\\right\\}^{-1} \\ .\n\\label{6} \\end{aligned}$$ For our purpose of the bound-state calculation is enough to know $\\tau^{(n)}(M_2)$ for $M_2 < 2M$ .\n\nThe momentum variables in the integral equation are the momenta in the null-plane for an on-mass-shell particle, $q^+$ and $q_\\perp$ [@fred92]. The transversal momentum is needed in three space-time dimensions.\n\nLet us discuss the limits of the variables $y=\\frac{q^+}{M_{3B}}$ and $q_\\perp$. In 1+1 space-time dimensions only the momentum fraction is enough to describe the spectator boson. The mass of the two-boson subsystem must be real and in 1+1 dimensions it implies $$\\begin{aligned}\n(M_2)^2\\ = \\ (M_{3B}-q^+)\\left(M_{3B}-\\frac{\nM^2}{q^+}\\right) \\ > \\ 0 \\ . \n\\label{7}\\end{aligned}$$ From the above inequality follows $ 1\\ > \\ y \\ > \\ \\frac{M^2}{M^2_{3B}} \\ . $\n\nIn 2+1 dimensions, we deduce the range of values of the perpendicular momentum allowed by the reality of the mass of the two-boson subsystem. Then $$\\begin{aligned}\n(M_2)^2\\ = \\ (M_{3B}-q^+)\\left(M_{3B}-\\frac{q^2_\\perp\n+M^2}{q^+}\\right)-q^2_\\perp \\ > \\ 0 \\ . \n\\label{8}\\end{aligned}$$ Solving the inequality for $q^2_\\perp$ , we obtain $ q^2_\\perp\\ < \\ (1-y)(M_{3B}^2y-M^2) \\ . $ The limits for $y$ are $ 1 \\ > \\  y \\ > \\ \\frac{M^2}{M^2_{3B}} \\ , $ and the lower bound comes from $q^2_\\perp \\ > \\ 0$.\n\nThe equation for the Faddeev component of the vertex in 1+1 space-time dimensions is obtained as the result of the $k^-$ integration in the momentum loop of Eq.(\\[1\\]). We also use Eq.(\\[5\\]) and the limit in the internal momentum fraction $x$ $$\\begin{aligned}\nv(y)=\\frac{i}{2\\pi}\\tau^{(2)}(M_2) \\int^{1-y}_\\frac{M^2}{M^2_{3B}}\\frac{dx}{x(1-y-x)}\n\\frac{v(x)}{M^2_{3B}-M^2_{03}},\n\\label{9}\\end{aligned}$$ where $(M_2)^2$ is given by Eq.(\\[7\\]) and the free mass of the virtual three-boson state in 1+1 dimensions is: $$\\begin{aligned}\nM^2_{03}=\n\\frac{M^2}{x}+\n\\frac{M^2}{y} + \\frac{M^2}{1-y-x} \\ .\n\\label{10}\\end{aligned}$$\n\nThe equation for the Faddeev component of the vertex in 2+1 space-time dimensions is found after the $k^-$ integration of in the momentum loop of Eq.(\\[1\\]), $$\\begin{aligned}\nv(y,\\vec q_\\perp)=\\pi^{-2}\n\\tau^{(3)}(M_2)\n\\int^{1-y}_\\frac{M^2}{M^2_{3B}}\\frac{dx}{x(1-y-x)}\n\\int^{k_\\perp^{max}}_{-k_\\perp^{max}}d^2k_\\perp\n\\frac{v(x,\\vec k_\\perp)}{M^2_{3B}-M^2_{03}},\n\\label{11}\\end{aligned}$$ where $M_2$ is given by Eq.(\\[8\\]), as well as $ k_\\perp^{max}=\\sqrt{(1-x)(M_{3B}^2x-M^2)}$ . The mass of the virtual three-boson state is: $$\\begin{aligned}\n M^2_{03}=\n\\frac{k^2_\\perp+M^2}{x}+\n\\frac{q^2_\\perp+M^2}{y} + \\frac{(q+k)^2_\\perp +M^2}{1-y-x} \\ .\n\\label{12}\\end{aligned}$$\n\nThe dependence of $v$ on $q^-$ is not specified because the spectator boson is on mass-shell. $q^+$ and $\\vec q_\\perp$ describe the spectator boson propagation. The relativistic equations in 1+1 and 2+1 dimensions, have a lower bound for the mass of the three-boson system which comes from the limits on the $x$ integration and the condition $y>\\frac{M^2}{M^2_{3B}}$ which implies $ M_{3B}> \\sqrt{2}M \\ . $ The same limit was obtained in 3+1 dimensions in [@fred92].\n\nThe Faddeev component of the ground state vertex, in 2+1 dimensions is rotationally symmetric in the x-y plane in Eq.(\\[11\\]). The mass of the boson gives the scale of the system. Here, the solution is presented for $M=1$. In Fig.(1), the numerical results for the ground-state binding energies $(E_{3B}=M_{2B}+M-M_{3B})$ of the three-boson system are shown in two and three-dimensions. In the nonrelativistic limit, for $E_{2B}=0$, the results approach the well-known values [@dodd].\n\nIn summary, we give an example of how null-plane dynamics can be elaborated. We develop a zero-range model of the three-boson bound state in the null-plane and solve numerically the dynamical equation for the ground-state in 1+1 and 2+1 space-time dimensions.\n\nThis work was supported by Conselho Nacional de Desenvolvimento e Pesquisa - CNPq and Funda\u00e7\u00e3o de Amparo a pesquisa do Estado de S\u00e3o Paulo - FAPESP.
