    "text": "NRS 395.001Definitions.As\nused in this chapter, unless the context otherwise requires, the words and\nterms defined in NRS 395.0065, 395.0075 and 395.008 have\nthe meanings ascribed to them in those sections.\n\nNRS 395.0065\u201cRelated services\u201d defined.\u201cRelated\nservices\u201d means room, board, transportation and such developmental, corrective\nand other supportive services, as may be required pursuant to minimum standards\nprescribed by the State Board of Education, to assist a person with a\ndisability to benefit from a special education program.\n\nNRS 395.008\u201cSpecial education program\u201d defined.\u201cSpecial\neducation program\u201d means a program which provides instruction specially\ndesigned in accordance with minimum standards prescribed by the State Board of\nEducation to meet the unique needs of persons with disabilities.\n\nNRS 395.010Special education program and related services to be provided to\nperson with disability.\n\n1. The Superintendent of Public\nInstruction shall provide a special education program and related services to\nall persons with disabilities who are eligible for benefits pursuant to this\nchapter.\n\n2. The Superintendent of Public\nInstruction may carry out the duties required by subsection 1 by:\n\n(a) Making arrangements with the governing body\nof any institution for persons with disabilities in any state having any such\ninstitution.\n\n(b) Placing the person with a disability in a\nfoster home or other residential facility, located in or outside of the school\ndistrict in which the person with a disability resides, that can provide an\nappropriate special education program and related services for the person\u2019s\nparticular disability. The Superintendent shall consider the recommendation of\nthe interagency panel in deciding where to place a person with a disability,\nbut the Superintendent has final authority regarding placement pursuant to this\nsubsection.\n\n(c) Making arrangements, if money from the\nFederal Government is available to cover the entire cost, for the unique\nspecial education and related services required to return students to this\nstate who have been placed in an institution outside of the State pursuant to\nthis chapter.\n\n3. The Superintendent of Public\nInstruction may make all necessary contracts, in accordance with any\nregulations the State Board of Examiners may prescribe, to carry out the\nprovisions of this section.\n\nNRS 395.020Eligibility for benefits.A\nperson with a disability is eligible to receive the benefits provided pursuant\nto this chapter if:\n\n1. The person is a resident of the State\nof Nevada;\n\n2. The person is under 22 years of age,\nexcept that where the enrollment period for the school year is before his or\nher 22nd birthday, the person remains eligible to complete that school year\nirrespective of his or her age;\n\n3. The Department of Education has\nprescribed minimum standards for the provision of a special education program\nand related services to persons with such a disability; and\n\n4. The person\u2019s school district:\n\n(a) Has prepared an appropriate plan for the\nindividualized education of the person with a disability; and\n\n(b) Is unable to provide an appropriate special\neducation program and related services for his or her particular disability and\ngrade or level of education.\n\n1. An adult person with a disability who\nis eligible to receive benefits pursuant to this chapter or a parent, guardian\nor other person having the care, custody or control of a person with a\ndisability who is eligible may file an application for those benefits with the\nboard of trustees of the school district in which the person with a disability\nis a resident.\n\n2. If the board of trustees is satisfied\nthat the school district is unable to provide an appropriate special education\nprogram and related services for the particular disability and grade or level\nof education of the person with a disability, the board shall certify that fact\nand transmit the application to the Superintendent of Public Instruction.\n\nNRS 395.040Duties of Superintendent of Public Instruction upon receipt of\napplication.\n\n1. Upon receipt and review of an\napplication for benefits, the Superintendent of Public Instruction may cause a\nmedical, psychological or educational examination of the person with a\ndisability to be conducted at state expense to determine the nature and extent\nof the disability.\n\n2. If the Superintendent of Public\nInstruction determines that the school district:\n\n(a) Has prepared an appropriate plan for the\nindividualized education of the person with a disability; and\n\n(b) Is unable to provide an appropriate special\neducation program and related services for the particular disability and grade\nor level of education of the person with a disability,\n\n\u00ca the\nSuperintendent shall make the arrangements for the provision of a special\neducation program and related services.\n\n3. The Superintendent of Public\nInstruction has final authority regarding the provision of a special education\nprogram and related services to any person with a disability.\n\nNRS 395.050Transportation of person with disability; State to pay for\nprovision of special education program and related services.\n\n1. When arrangements for the provision of\na special education program and related services to a person with a disability\nhave been completed by the Superintendent of Public Instruction, the\nSuperintendent shall advise the board of trustees of the school district to\nmake provision, at the expense of the school district, for transporting the\nperson with a disability to a place designated by the Superintendent. The\nSuperintendent shall make necessary arrangements for transporting the person\nwith a disability from the designated place to the institution, foster home or\nother residential facility and return to the designated place at the expense of\nthe State.\n\n2. The provision of a special education\nprogram and related services to a person with a disability pursuant to this\nchapter must be paid by the State without any charge to the person with a\ndisability or to a parent, guardian or other person having the care, custody or\ncontrol of the person with a disability.\n\nNRS 395.060Money to carry out provisions of chapter.Money to carry out the provisions of this\nchapter may be provided by direct legislative appropriation from the State\nGeneral Fund, federal grants or any other source of money made available for\nthat purpose.\n\n[470:32:1956]\u2014(NRS A 1981, 1043)\n\nNRS 395.070Interagency Panel: Responsibility; membership; duties.\n\n1. The Interagency Panel is hereby\ncreated. The Panel is responsible for making recommendations concerning the\nplacement of persons with disabilities who are eligible to receive benefits\npursuant to this chapter. The Panel consists of:\n\n(a) The Administrator of the Division of Child\nand Family Services of the Department of Health and Human Services;\n\n(b) The Administrator of the Division of Public\nand Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human Services;\n\n(c) The Director of the Department of Health and\nHuman Services; and\n\n(d) The Superintendent of Public Instruction.\n\n2. A member of the Panel may designate a\nperson to represent him or her at any meeting of the Panel. The person\ndesignated may exercise all the duties, rights and privileges of the member he\nor she represents.\n\n3. The Panel shall:\n\n(a) Every time a person with a disability is to\nbe placed pursuant to subsection 2 of NRS 395.010\nin a foster home or residential facility, meet to determine the needs of the\nperson and the availability of homes or facilities under the authority of the\nDepartment of Health and Human Services after a joint evaluation of that person\nis completed by the Department of Education and the Department of Health and\nHuman Services;\n\n(b) Determine the appropriate placement of the\nperson, giving priority to homes or facilities under the authority of the\nDepartment of Health and Human Services over any home or facility located\noutside of this State; and\n\nNRS 395.080Priority of placement in homes or facilities located in this\nState.Persons with disabilities\nwho are entitled to benefits pursuant to this chapter must be given priority of\nplacement in homes or facilities which are located in this State and under the\nauthority of the Department of Health and Human Services.\n\nNRS 395.090Monitoring of children placed in foster homes and residential\nfacilities outside State.The\nDivision of Child and Family Services of the Department of Health and Human\nServices shall, when monitoring children under its authority whom it has placed\nin foster homes and residential facilities outside of the State, also monitor the\nwell-being of the persons with disabilities who have been placed in those\nfoster homes and residential facilities pursuant to this chapter, and report to\nthe Superintendent of Public Instruction concerning the condition of those\npersons with disabilities."
