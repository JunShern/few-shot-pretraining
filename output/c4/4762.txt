Learn more about creating an eTaxBC account.\nIf you\u2019re a registered accommodation provider located within a participating MRDT area, and you haven't signed up for an eTaxBC account, you\u2019ll receive a paper MRDT return by mail at the end of each reporting period. If you enrol with eTaxBC to file your returns online, you may consolidate your MRDT accounts onto one MRDT return.\nYour completed MRDT return and payment must be received on or before the last day of the month following the end of the reporting period.\nYou use the MRDT return to calculate and pay the MRDT collected on your sales of accommodation during your reporting period.\nYou must file an MRDT return even if you didn\u2019t collect MRDT during the reporting period.\nDon\u2019t use your MRDT return to pay any other amounts owed to us, such as any amounts owing from an audit or fees for a dishonoured cheque. In these cases, you will be sent a separate notice that includes payment instructions.\nIf you're registered to collect PST on accommodation located within a participating MRDT area (PDF), you must file both an MRDT return and a Provincial Sales Tax Return (FIN 400) (PDF).\nThis means you report the PST on the PST return and you report the MRDT on the MRDT Return. While PST and MRDT must be reported on separate returns, you may remit a single payment to cover both returns. Read the Guide to Completing the PST Return.\nEnter all amounts in Canadian dollars.\nEnter the total amount of all of your accommodation sales for the period including taxable, non-taxable and exempt sales. Don't include any tax.\nEnter the total amount of MRDT collectable (either collected, or charged but not collected) on all taxable accommodation sales.\nInclude any tax you may have collected in error. If your customer is entitled to a refund, see Box D below for more information.\nNote: MRDT returns are not eligible for commission.\nYou must keep supporting documentation for any adjustments you claim. We may contact you to request documentation to verify the adjustment amounts claimed or they may be requested during an audit.\nEnter the amount of MRDT remitted (paid) on accommodation sold on credit where the amount payable was later deemed uncollectable (a bad debt) and written off.\nYou must claim this adjustment in the same reporting period that the amount payable was written off. If you don\u2019t claim the adjustment in the same reporting period, you must apply for a refund. You must apply for the refund within four years of the date the amount payable was written off. Learn more about bad debts in Bulletin CTB 001, Bad Debts (PDF).\nEnter the amount of any MRDT that you\u2019ve credited or refunded to your customers but have previously remitted to us. If you don\u2019t claim the adjustment in the same reporting period, you must apply for a refund. You must apply for the refund within four years of the date the amount of MRDT was refunded or credited to your customer.\nNote: If a customer paid MRDT on accommodation that was occupied for a continuous period of 27 days or more (or a continuous period of more than one month if before October 1, 2018), the customer must apply to us for a refund. You may not issue a refund to the customer and claim the amount as an adjustment unless the amount was charged in error at the time of payment.\nLearn more about exempt sales of accommodation (PDF).\nEnter the total of the amounts claimed in Box C and Box D. If you\u2019re using eTaxBC this box is calculated for you.\nEnter the total of the amount from Box B minus the amount from Box E.
