The town centres offer a variety of caf\u00e9s, and the smaller townships are also worth visiting to spend a relaxed summer day while enjoying a cup of coffee and delicious pastries or wonderfully refreshing ice cream. Some of the places well worth visiting are: the Lankosken Myllykahvila caf\u00e9 in Merikarvia, the Onnenkoski caf\u00e9 in Lamppi, the Kauttua ironworks in Eura, summer caf\u00e9 Linturi in K\u00f6yli\u00f6, and the Villil\u00e4 summer caf\u00e9 in Nakkila.\nThe Villa Linturi summer caf\u00e9 welcomes summer guests by the K\u00f6yli\u00f6ntie road in the village of Kepola in S\u00e4kyl\u00e4. All Kaino products are designed and manufactured on-site from start to finish.\nCaf\u00e9 Ky\u00f6peli in Ruukinpuisto park in Kauttua is a summer caf\u00e9 operated by the 4-H Club in Eura. The caf\u00e9 provides delicacies both sweet and savoury, sells hand-made products from local artisans, and offers varying art exhibitions.\nThe caf\u00e9 also has a tourist info point for the ironworks area. Caf\u00e9 Ky\u00f6peli is open June 6 \u2013 August 13, Tue-Fri between 11am\u20135pm, Sat between 10am\u20133pm, and Sun between noon\u20134pm. The caf\u00e9 is closed on Mondays.\nLearn about the diverse summer programme of Ruukinpuiston Kes\u00e4 and plan your trip to the fabulous cultural vistas it offers. Lunch is served at the Club on weekdays between 11am and 2pm.\nThe charming Old Pappila (Old Parsonage in Finnish) in Huittinen acts these days as a banquet restaurant with the exception of summer lunch, available at the atmospheric parsonage from late June until early August. See the Pappila Web site for more information about the lunch service.\nThe caf\u00e9 of Lankosken Kahvimylly operates in the old mill built in 1835 in Merikarvia. Upstairs you\u2019ll find the gift shop for some perfect presents to bring back home.
